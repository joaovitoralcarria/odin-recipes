Lasagna

Description

This very tasty recipe is one of the most delicious and easy to make.

The lasagna is a classic and comforting dish that combines succulent layers of pasta, assorted fillings and flavorful sauces. With its vibrant colors and harmonious flavors, it is a sensory experience that brings people together around the table. In addition to being delicious, lasagna evokes affectionate memories and represents togetherness and warmth, becoming a symbol of comforting cuisine.

Lasagna Ingredients

  • Meat: This super meaty lasagna has sweet italian sausage and lean ground beef.
  • Onion and Garlic: An onion and two cloves of garlic are cooked with the meeat to add tons of flavor.
  • Tomato Products: You'll need a can of crushed tomatoes, two cans of tomato sauce, and two cans of tomato paste.
  • Sugar: Two tablespoons of white sugar add subtle sweetness and enhance the flavor of the sauce.
  • Spices and Seasonings: This lasagna recipe is flavored with fresh parsley, dried basel leaves, salt, Italian seasoning, fennel seeds, and black pepper.
  • Lasagna Noodles: Use store-bought or homemade lasagna noodles.
  • Cheeses: Parmesan, mozzarella, and ricotta cheese make this lasagna extra decadent.
  • Egg: An egg helps bind the ricotta so it doesn't ooze out of the lasagna when you cut into it.

How to Make Lasagna Step-By-Step

You can follow this steps to make the lasagna:

  1. Make the meat sauce.
  2. Cook the noodles.
  3. Make the ricotta mixture.
  4. Layer the lasagna according to the recipe instructions.
  5. Cover with foil and brake.
  6. Let the lasagna rest before saving.

How to Layer Lasagna:

  1. Meat sauce.
  2. Noodles.
  3. Ricotta mixture.
  4. Mozzarella slices.
  5. Meat sauce.
  6. Parmesan cheese.
  7. Repeat the layers, then top with the remaining Parmesan.

How Long to Cook the Lasagna:

The assembled lasagna should take about 50 minutes to cook in an oven preheated to 375 degrees F. Cover it with foil for the first 25 minutes, then let it cook unconvered for the final 25 minutes. Also, it's important to let the lasagna rest at room temperature for about 15 minutes before you cut into it.
